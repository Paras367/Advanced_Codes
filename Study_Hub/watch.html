Coulomb's Law:
The electrostatic force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance.
F = k · q₁q₂ / r²   where k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²
Electric Field (E):
Force per unit positive test charge placed at a point.
E = F/q₀ = kq/r²   (in N/C or V/m)
Key Points to Remember:
  • Coulomb's constant k = 1/(4πε₀)
  • ε₀ = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² C²/N·m²
  • Force is along the line joining charges
  • Like charges repel, unlike charges attract
