What happens if you drop a feather and a hammer on the Moon?

Before diving into equations and formulas, let's engage your natural curiosity. Think about this scenario: An astronaut stands on the Moon, holding a feather in one hand and a hammer in the other. They release both at exactly the same time. What do you think happens?

Take a moment to consider:

  • Which one hits the ground first?
  • Why might they behave differently than on Earth?
  • What forces are at play?

Newton's Second Law: F = ma

You've discovered a fundamental principle of physics! When air resistance is removed (like on the Moon), all objects fall at the same rate regardless of their mass. This is because:

F = m × a

Net Force = Mass × Acceleration

On the Moon, there's no air resistance. Only gravity acts on both objects equally (acceleration due to gravity is constant). Since F = ma, and the gravitational force is proportional to mass, the mass cancels out, resulting in the same acceleration for all objects!
