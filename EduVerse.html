üß† Concept Summary

Newton's First Law (Inertia): An object stays at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force. This is why you jerk forward when a car suddenly brakes!

Newton's Second Law (F=ma): The acceleration of an object depends on the net force acting upon it and its mass. Mathematically: F = ma

Newton's Third Law (Action-Reaction): For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction. When you push a wall, it pushes back with equal force!

üìå Derivation: F = dp/dt ‚Üí F = ma ‚ñº

Starting with momentum: p = mv

Newton's Second Law states: F = dp/dt

For constant mass: F = d(mv)/dt = m(dv/dt) = ma

Hence, F = ma when mass is constant.

üéØ Free Body Diagrams (FBD) ‚ñº

A Free Body Diagram shows all forces acting on an object:

  • Weight (W): Always acts downward = mg
  • Normal Force (N): Perpendicular to surface
  • Tension (T): Along rope/string
  • Friction (f): Opposes motion

Key: Isolate the object and draw ALL forces as arrows from the center.
